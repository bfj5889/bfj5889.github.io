Mario Castellanos

A multifaceted visionary who fuses the tangible virtues of a physician, a researcher, an innovator, an entrepreneur and a gentleman into a unique and powerful champion for the company. Senior clinical care and research medical professional with more than 20 years of experience in primary care, research, scholarly and teaching activities. A challanger who pushes the boundaries; high drive, passion and creativity - these are features that inspire his peers, residents, students, and now Innovene.

As a researcher, Dr. Castellanos has remained focused on the human papillomavirus (HPV) infection and associated cancers, and his two main discoveries include the identification of synthetic peptides that can induce a cytotoxic T-cell immune response against HPV type 18 targets, and a novel method to improve the histopathologic classification of HPV (+) cervical precancers, advancing both therapeutics and diagnostics.

His proven track record in development and implementation of innovative solutions across a range of areas has resulted in the award of a patent for a Histochemical Method to Identify and Predict Disease Progression of HPV-infected Lesions, and having yet another patent in the pipeline for Activity Enhancing Curcumin Composition and Methods of Use, as a novel treatment for HPV associated disease.

Dr. Castellanos’ leadership in conducting important and impactful research has been proved by awarding him multiple grants for clinical research projects. In 2009, Dr. Castellanos was appointed the Director of Research for the Department of Medicine at Staten Island University Hospital, North Shore-LIJ Health System. He has led initiatives to establish collaborations between scientist and physicians, accelerating laboratory advances into the clinical setting. As a physician, Dr. Castellanos has remained very active clinically. He conducts weekly medical continuity clinics and a medical women’s health session.

Being a dedicated scholar, Dr. Castellanos is the first or senior author in about 50 publications in prestigious journals and was an invited presenter at more than 20 international and national conferences. His academic activity as a faculty was recognized by his recent promotion to the position of Research Associate Professor, Department of Medicine SUNY Health Science Center at Brooklyn, New York. He is a member of several notable professional societies such as The International Papillomavirus Society, European Organization Genital Infection and Neoplasia, and the American College of Physicians. Dr. Castellanos graduated from an accelerated 7 year B.S.-M.D. Program of Sophie Davis School of Biomedical Education and Albany Medical College followed by Post-Doctoral Research Fellowship in Weill Cornell Medical College and Medical Residency & Women's Health Fellowship at Staten Island University Hospital.

This broad-spectrum activity has resulted in institution of INNOVENE, a company of which he is the President and Co-founder, a company with the goal of catalyzing traditional research and developing plant based therapeutic compounds. The company is seeking to introduce its proprietary botanical compound TriCurin in to the cancer prevention and treatment marketplace, as the first treatment for cervical HPV infection and associated cancers.
