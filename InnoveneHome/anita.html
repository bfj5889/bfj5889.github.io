Anita Szerszen

CO-FOUNDER AND DIRECTOR, CLINICAL DEVELOPMENT

Education

Poland, educated in the USA, New York College of Osteopathic Medicine, PhD program in Developmental Biology and Molecular Genetics at Rutgers, the State University of New Jersey.

Academic Appointment

Assistant Professor of Medicine, SUNY Health Center at Brooklyn

Current Position

An attending Board certified physician in internal medicine and geriatrics Chair, Institutional Review Board North Shore-LIJ Health System Director of Research, Geriatric Medicine Division, Staten Island University Hospital.

Research

Worked on the frontiers of research on Green Fluorescent Protein, the most recognizable reporter of gene expression when working in Dr. William W. Ward’s lab. Associate Director of Geriatrics Research, Staten Island University Hospital-Northwell Health. Recognized clinical scientist. Developed and characterized mutant green fluorescent protein (GFP). Her lab collaborated with Dr. Chalfie a Nobel prize laureate in Chemistry 2008.

Role in Innovene

In collaboration with other key team members, Anita takes part in defining the overall business strategy and direction of the company. In particular, Anita is responsible for the clinical planning in efforts to translate the bench research findings into the clinical practice, leading and implementing the clinical direction for Innovene. As Anita is in charge of the regulatory process associated with clinical trials, she leads efforts to secure the IND and function as a liaison between Innovene, FDA, and the IRB, being directly involved in planning and execution of future clinical trials.

Scientist

As true a scientist as can be, she considers any project a venture into research rather than business, with failure being as useful information as success.
